## Introduction

Venice Beach transforms into a laid-back holiday haven where street drummers share the boardwalk with seasonal carolers, and palm-lined canals shimmer with residential light displays that rival any suburban cul-de-sac. This bohemian beachside neighborhood embraces the holidays with its signature eclectic flair‚Äîthink outdoor ice skating near the pier in Santa Monica, holiday shopping along the "coolest block in America" (Abbot Kinney Boulevard), and the Venice Canals illuminated by twinkling lights reflecting off tranquil waterways.

Unlike Beverly Hills' polished glamour, Venice keeps it real: local artisans selling handcrafted gifts at Artists & Fleas, restaurants like Gjelina serving seasonal California cuisine on communal wooden tables, and the iconic Venice Sign lit up against purple December sunsets. From the Marina del Rey Holiday Boat Parade's festive flotilla to sand berms for beach sledding, Venice proves you can celebrate Christmas with your toes in the sand and still catch all the holiday magic.

**Insider Tip:** Park near 25th Street on weekday evenings to explore the Venice Canals holiday lights without weekend crowds. The display is free, self-paced, and best experienced after sundown through early January.

## Eat

### Breakfast & Brunch

Start your day at **Gjusta**, the deli-style eatery that locals love for early morning runs before the brunch rush hits. Smoked fish platters, fresh pastries baked on-site, and their outdoor patio provide a hidden gem experience where you'll rub elbows with neighborhood residents rather than tourists (gjelinagroup.com). Arrive before 9am on weekends to avoid lines that stretch down the block.

**Dudley Market** serves toasts and mimosas that have become a semi-well-kept secret among Venice insiders. Their coffee is excellent, and the vibe is relaxed California casual‚Äîperfect fuel before exploring the boardwalk (dudleymarket.com).

For classic beachside breakfast, **The Rose Venice** (formerly Rose Cafe) has been an integral part of neighborhood life since 1979. Chef Jason Neroni helms this sprawling food compound at 220 Rose Ave, serving everything from morning pastries to hearty brunch plates. Named one of the 22 greatest restaurants in Los Angeles by OpenTable (gjelinagroup.com).

### Lunch & Casual Dining

**Gjelina** opened on Abbot Kinney Boulevard in 2008 and became "the mother of the genre" for all-day California caf√©s. The restaurant's wooden communal tables, industrial chic stools, and effortlessly stylish crowds embody the fantasy of California cool. Creative pizzas (don't miss the anchovy pizza) alongside vegetable-forward small plates make this consistently one of the best restaurants in Los Angeles. Gjelina is featured in the 2025 MICHELIN Guide USA (gjelina.com).

## Do

### Major Holiday Events

**Venice Winter Fest**
**Saturday, November 22, 2025 | 10:00am - 6:00pm**
Venice Blvd transforms into a winter wonderland with artisan markets, live music, and festive food. Interactive winter-themed activities designed for all ages make this family-friendly event a kickoff to Venice's holiday season. Local vendors showcase handcrafted gifts, jewelry, and art‚Äîperfect for finding unique presents you won't see on Rodeo Drive (thevenicefest.com).

**Venice Canals Holiday Lights**
**Late November 2025 - Early January 2026 | Best viewed after sundown**
Residents along the Venice Canals decorate their homes, boats, yards, and bridges with festive lights, transforming this historic neighborhood into a magical, twinkling wonderland. It's completely free to visit, and you can stroll the pedestrian walkways at your own pace. Park near 25th Street and prepare to be dazzled by the shimmering displays reflecting off the calm canal waters. The neighborhood gets busy on weekends, so weekday evenings offer a more peaceful experience (visitveniceca.com).

**Venice Canals Christmas Boat Parade**
**[TEMPLATE: First Saturday or Sunday of December, ~4:30pm start]**
The annual Venice Canals Christmas Boat Parade features participants decorating small boats with lights, holiday figures, and sound systems, creating a mini-fleet of Christmas cheer as they glide down the canals. This intimate neighborhood tradition offers a charming alternative to the larger Marina del Rey parade. Typically starts as the sun sets around 4:30pm at the intersection of Eastern and Carroll Canals (visitveniceca.com).

**December 15, 2025 - January 4, 2026**

**Marina del Rey Holiday Boat Parade (63rd Annual)**
**Saturday, December 13, 2025**
Burton Chace Park festivities run 4:00pm-8:00pm, with a dazzling light show from 5:45pm-6:00pm (15 minutes), followed by the parade from 6:00pm-8:00pm. This year's theme is "A Nightmare Before Christmas." The harbor lights up with festively decorated boats competing for best holiday display. Best viewing spots include Burton Chace Park (13650 Mindanao Way), Fisherman's Village (13755 Fiji Way) with waterfront dining, or charter cruises for prime viewing. ### Year-Round Venice Experiences (Enhanced in Winter)

**Venice Beach Boardwalk** ‚Äî The legendary Ocean Front Walk is less crowded in winter, making it easier to appreciate the street performers, artists, Muscle Beach outdoor gym, and eclectic shops. The weekend drum circle continues through December weather permitting.

**Venice Fishing Pier** at the end of Washington Street offers ocean views and fishing opportunities with fewer crowds than summer. Sunset from the pier is particularly stunning in winter's golden light.

**The Mosaic Tile House** ‚Äî This whimsical residence with eye-catching exterior adorned with mirrors and intricate hand-crafted tiles covering every inch is only open for tours on Saturdays [TEMPLATE: booking details, pricing].

**The Venice Walk Streets** ‚Äî These pedestrian-only streets inland from the beach date back to the 1900s and were part of Abbot Kinney's original vision. Stroll Nowita Place, Strongs Drive, or Dell Avenue to see residential Venice decorated for the holidays away from tourist crowds.

## Shop

### Abbot Kinney Boulevard

Dubbed "the coolest block in America" by GQ, Abbot Kinney Boulevard (stretching from Venice Boulevard to Pacific Avenue) is a Boho chic shopping destination featuring one-of-a-kind local goods and internationally recognized brands (abbotkinneyblvd.com).

**Burro** ‚Äî Step inside and find everything from cards and candles to clothing, coffee table books and jewelry, all with a California aesthetic. Perfect for curating unique holiday gifts.

**Bazar** ‚Äî One of the longest-standing shops on Abbot Kinney and one of the most special. Owner Tina Wakino keeps the space packed with antique furniture, textiles, and racks of hard-to-find international brands and vintage pieces for both men and women.

**Heist** ‚Äî A long-standing staple that has evolved and expanded with Venice. This large, modern boutique brims with labels both well-loved and still-unknown.

**Ruti** ‚Äî Designs and curates unique ready-to-wear clothes, shoes, bags and accessories with bohemian flair.

**Independently** ‚Äî Owned women's clothing boutique with books, crystals, jewelry, hats, art and gifts. Perfect embodiment of Venice's bohemian vibe.

## Takeaway

Venice Beach's holiday season delivers bohemian cool where street culture meets seasonal celebration, and you can watch boats decorated with Christmas lights while feeling the ocean breeze. From the intimate Venice Canals illuminated by residential displays to the festive Marina del Rey parade, this neighborhood proves Los Angeles does coastal Christmas with style.

**Pack smart:** Layers for cool December evenings (temperatures drop after sunset), comfortable walking shoes for canal strolls and boardwalk exploration, and a backpack for carrying finds from Abbot Kinney boutiques. Bring a reusable water bottle and maybe a beach blanket‚Äîyou're in Venice, after all.

**Pro tip:** Start your day with coffee at Gjusta before 9am, explore the boardwalk in late morning when performers are active but crowds are manageable, hit Abbot Kinney for afternoon shopping (stores open around 11am), catch sunset from the Venice Pier, then stroll the Venice Canals after dark for maximum holiday light magic. Park on side streets like 18th Avenue or Mildred Avenue (free but fills fast).

**Venice vs. Beverly Hills:** Where Beverly Hills delivers polished luxury, Venice offers authentic bohemia. You'll trade champagne at the Peninsula for craft coffee at Gjusta, Rodeo Drive for Abbot Kinney, and orchestrated perfection for creative chaos. Both are quintessentially Los Angeles; choose based on your vibe.
