## Introduction


Pasadena transforms into a Victorian holiday wonderland each winter, where century-old traditions meet contemporary cultural celebrations. Known worldwide for hosting the Tournament of Roses Parade on New Year's Day, this historic city begins its festivities weeks earlier with elaborate tree lightings, world-class museum exhibitions and dining experiences set against a backdrop of Craftsman architecture and California sunshine (cityofpasadena.net). 

The charming Old Town district becomes a pedestrian-friendly celebration hub with twinkling lights strung between buildings, while the nearby Arroyo Seco and Rose Bowl create additional gathering spaces for community festivities (pasadenanow.com). From elegant afternoon teas at The Langham Huntington to family-friendly activities at Kidspace Museum, Pasadena offers a refined yet accessible holiday experience steeped in Southern California tradition (visitpasadena.com).

**Insider Tip:** For the best Tournament of Roses Parade experience, arrive by 6am on January 1st with folding chairs and blankets. The section of Colorado Boulevard between Sierra Madre Villa and Hill Avenue offers excellent views without the massive crowds near Old Town. Many locals camp out overnight on December 31st - it's legal and part of the tradition. Alternatively, purchase reserved grandstand seats months in advance through tournamentofroses.com.

### Major Holiday Events


**Pasadena Tree Lighting Ceremony**
**Late November 2025 (typically Friday before Thanksgiving) | 5:30pm - 8:00pm**
Pasadena City Hall (100 N Garfield Ave) marks the official start of the holiday season with live performances from local schools, caroling and the illumination of a towering tree adorned with thousands of lights. Free event with hot chocolate and festive activities for families (cityofpasadena.net).

**Enchanted Forest of Light at Descanso Gardens**
**November 15, 2025 - January 12, 2026 | Nightly 5:30pm - 10:00pm**
Just north of Pasadena in La CaÃ±ada Flintridge (1418 Descanso Dr), visitors walk through illuminated forest paths with interactive art installations, projected light displays, and themed gardens transformed by millions of LED lights. Timed-entry tickets required; $32-42 for adults, $18-22 for children. Purchase in advance at descansogardens.org.

**Tournament of Roses Parade**
**Wednesday, January 1, 2026 | 8:00am start**
One of America's most iconic New Year's Day traditions, featuring elaborate floral floats, marching bands from around the world, and equestrian units processing down Colorado Boulevard. Reserved grandstand seats ($70-110) available at tournamentofroses.com. Free curb-side viewing requires arriving by 6am with chairs and blankets.

### Cultural Attractions


**The Norton Simon Museum** (411 W Colorado Blvd) offers a serene holiday experience with world-class European and Asian art collections displayed in sculpture gardens decorated with seasonal touches. Open Wed-Mon 12pm-5pm; $15 adults, $12 seniors, free for students and under 18. Extended December hours and special evening programs (nortonsimon.org).

**The Huntington Library, Art Museum and Botanical Gardens** (1151 Oxford Rd, San Marino) presents breathtaking holiday displays with the historic mansion decked in period decorations and 120 acres of themed gardens perfect for winter strolls. Open daily 10am-5pm; $29-38 adults, $24-32 seniors, $13-16 children. Advance timed-entry tickets required (huntington.org).

**Kidspace Children's Museum** (480 N Arroyo Blvd) in Brookside Park offers holiday-themed programming including craft workshops and seasonal storytimes. Open daily 10am-5pm; $14 general admission (kidspacemuseum.org).

### Ongoing Holiday Activities

**Old Town Pasadena Holiday Events** - Throughout December, free concerts and holiday markets pop up along Colorado Boulevard and One Colorado courtyard, with local artisans setting up shop (oldpasadena.org).

**Rose Bowl Flea Market** - Second Sunday of each month including December 8, 2025. Vendors sell vintage clothing, furniture, jewelry and holiday dÃ©cor; 9am-3pm, $12 admission after 9am, $20 for early bird 7-9am (rgcshows.com).

## Stay

### Luxury Hotels

**The Langham Huntington, Pasadena** sits on 23 acres at the base of the San Gabriel Mountains (1401 S Oak Knoll Ave), offering a historic resort experience with multiple dining options, a full-service spa, and holiday packages that include breakfast and special amenities. Rooms from $350-650/night during holidays. Call (626) 568-3900 or visit langhamhotels.com.

**The Westin Pasadena** (191 N Los Robles Ave) provides central location near the Convention Center and Old Town shopping district, with spacious rooms and easy access to holiday events. Rooms from $200-400/night (marriott.com).

### Boutique Properties

**The Bissell House Bed & Breakfast** (201 Orange Grove Ave) provides Victorian elegance in a carefully restored 1887 mansion, where guests enjoy gourmet breakfasts and personalized service just blocks from Old Town. Rooms from $250-450/night; (626) 441-3535 (bissellhouse.com).

**The Saga Motor Hotel** (1633 E Colorado Blvd) in the Pasadena Playhouse District features renovated mid-century modern design with walkability to dining and entertainment. Rooms from $150-280/night (sagapasadena.com).

## Shop

### Old Town Pasadena Shopping

Old Town Pasadena transforms into a shopper's paradise during the holidays, with Colorado Boulevard hosting over 200 retailers from national brands to independent boutiques (oldpasadena.org). Woman's Bookstore, one of the oldest feminist bookstores in the nation, offers curated titles and hosts author events throughout December (womansbookstore.com). Nearby, Vroman's Bookstore anchors the district with three floors of books, gifts and a cafÃ©, making it a popular destination for holiday browsing and gift-wrapping services (vromansbookstore.com).

For unique finds, explore the artisan vendors at Jackalope Fair, which sometimes pops up in Pasadena during December, featuring handcrafted goods from hundreds of makers (jackalopefair.com). The Pasadena Antique Mall provides vintage treasures and collectibles for those seeking one-of-a-kind gifts (pasadenaantiquedesigncenter.com). South Lake Avenue offers another shopping corridor with national retailers and local boutiques, all decorated for the season (southlakedistrict.com).

The Rose Bowl Flea Market, held on the second Sunday of each month, continues through winter with vendors selling vintage clothing, furniture, jewelry and holiday dÃ©cor; the December edition is particularly festive (rgcshows.com). Paseo Colorado, an open-air shopping center in the heart of Pasadena, hosts holiday events and provides covered walkways connecting specialty shops and restaurants (paseocoloradopasadena.com). For food gifts, Nicole's Gourmet Foods offers artisanal provisions, prepared meals and holiday catering that showcase local California ingredients (nicolesgourmetfoods.com).

## Takeaway

Pasadena's holiday season blends historic elegance with contemporary California style, offering visitors a refined yet relaxed celebration. From world-class museums and botanical gardens to Victorian architecture and New Year's parade traditions, this Crown City provides cultural depth alongside festive cheer. Pack layers for cool evenings, comfortable shoes for exploring Old Town and a camera to capture the Tournament of Roses preparations that make Pasadena a legendary Southern California destination each winter.
