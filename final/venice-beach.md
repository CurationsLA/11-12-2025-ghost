# Venice Beach Christmas & Holiday Guide 2025

**The CurationsLA Christmas and Holiday Guide to Venice Beach**

*A surf-town Christmas where canals shimmer with lights and Santa swaps reindeer for skateboards*

---

## Introduction

Venice Beach celebrates the holidays with palm trees draped in lights and a laid-back, bohemian vibe that makes Christmas feel uniquely Californian. The famed Venice Canals become a centerpiece of festive magic as residents decorate bridges and kayaks for the annual boat parade, creating an enchanting evening spectacle that draws visitors from across Los Angeles (venicebeachhouse.com). 

Along the iconic boardwalk, street performers incorporate holiday themes into their acts, while shoppers browse local vendors for handmade gifts and artisan crafts. The neighborhood's creative spirit shines during the winter season, with the Venice Winter Fest and holiday sign lighting bringing the community together for celebration (business.venicechamber.net).

With mild winter weather, stunning sunsets over the Pacific, and Abbot Kinney Boulevard's eclectic boutiques staying open for holiday shopping, Venice offers a perfect blend of beachside relaxation and festive cheer. This is where you can ice your coffee in December and still feel the holiday magic in the salt air and twinkling lights along the canals.

**Insider Tip:** The best time to visit the Venice Canals during the holidays is just after sunset, when the decorated houses and bridges light up, creating magical reflections on the water. Park near Washington Boulevard and walk along the canal paths for the most photogenic views.

## Eat

### Breakfast & Brunch

Start your holiday morning at **The Butcher's Daughter**, a stylish all-day vegetarian caf√© on Abbot Kinney Boulevard known for fresh cold-pressed juices, avocado toast, and beautiful a√ßai bowls. The bright, plant-filled space feels like a wellness retreat with holiday touches during December. Open daily 8am-9pm; expect $15-22 for breakfast dishes (thebutchersdaughter.com).

**Gjusta**, the acclaimed deli-style bakery on Sunset Avenue, serves exceptional smoked-fish platters, wood-fired bagels, seasonal pastries, and house-made breads that draw lines out the door. Arrive before 10am to beat the crowds, or call ahead for takeout at (310) 314-0320. Brunch items range from $12-28 (gjusta.com).

**Rose Cafe** on Rose Avenue offers all-day dining in a garden courtyard setting, with breakfast classics, market-driven salads, and excellent coffee. The relaxed atmosphere and heated outdoor seating make it perfect for holiday mornings; $14-26 for breakfast and brunch dishes (rosecafevenice.com).

### Lunch & Dinner

**Gjelina** on Abbot Kinney has become a Venice institution, serving wood-fired pizzas, seasonal vegetables, and California-Mediterranean dishes family-style. The industrial-chic space with an open kitchen and communal tables creates a festive atmosphere during the holidays. Reservations essential; call (310) 450-1429. Expect $18-38 for mains (gjelina.com).

**Felix Trattoria**, from the Gjelina group, focuses on handmade pastas and Italian classics in a more refined setting. Their holiday menu often features special preparations of seasonal ingredients like winter truffles and squash. Pastas $24-32, mains $32-48 (felixla.com).

**Gran Blanco**, a modern Mexican restaurant and mezcal bar, serves innovative coastal Mexican cuisine with fresh ceviches, tacos, and creative cocktails. The upscale-casual vibe and excellent tequila selection make it ideal for holiday celebrations; $16-36 for entrees (granblancola.com).

### Bars & Hidden Gems

**The Whaler** is a casual beachfront bar overlooking the ocean, perfect for sunset cocktails while watching the holiday crowds on the boardwalk. The no-frills atmosphere and reasonable prices ($10-14 cocktails) make it a local favorite (thewhalervenice.com).

**Belles Beach House**, a retro Tiki-style bar with Hawaiian-inspired izakaya dishes, serves hand-crafted tropical cocktails in a laid-back setting decorated with vintage surfboards and island memorabilia. Happy hour runs 4-7pm daily; cocktails $12-16 (bellesbeachhouse.com).

**Hidden Gem:** **Only The Wild Ones** is a hi-fi natural wine bar where analog vinyl records spin constantly, creating an intimate atmosphere for conversation. The carefully curated natural wine list and small bites menu make it a sophisticated hideaway. Natural wines by the glass $12-18, small plates $10-18 (onlythewildones.com).

**Local's Secret:** **The Townhouse** on Windward Circle has been Venice's dive bar since 1915, with dark wood, pool tables, and locals who've been coming here for decades. It's the antithesis of Venice's trendy scene, and that's exactly why insiders love it. Cash only; well drinks $8-10.

## Do

### Major Holiday Events

**Venice Winter Fest**
**Friday, November 22, 2025 | 4:00pm - 10:00pm**
Venice Boulevard transforms into a winter wonderland with a unique "retro ski-lodge meets surf-groove" theme. Over 300 local creators and artisans sell handcrafted goods, while food trucks serve seasonal fare and live bands provide entertainment. Take festive photos with Santa (who might be wearing board shorts), participate in the ugliest holiday sweater contest, and enjoy mulled wine from local vendors. Free admission; located on Venice Boulevard between Lincoln Boulevard and Pacific Avenue (eventbrite.com/venicewinterfest).

**Venice Holiday Sign Lighting**
**Friday, December 6, 2025 | 5:30pm - 8:00pm**
The historic "VENICE" sign on Windward Avenue gets bathed in red and green holiday lights during this community celebration. The event features live music from local bands, food vendors, and family-friendly activities including face painting and balloon artists. The illuminated sign becomes an Instagram-worthy backdrop for the rest of the season. Free event (business.venicechamber.net).

**Venice Canals Holiday Boat Parade**
**Mid-December 2025 (typically second Saturday) | 6:00pm - 8:00pm**
This beloved neighborhood tradition sees residents decorating small boats, kayaks, canoes, and paddleboards with thousands of holiday lights, then floating through the Venice Canals while neighbors line the banks to watch. Some boats feature holiday music, costumed participants, and elaborate themes. The parade typically starts at the Grand Canal near Washington Boulevard. Arrive early to secure a viewing spot along the canal paths. Free to watch; check venicecanalshouseboat.com for exact date confirmation.

**Marina del Rey 63rd Annual Holiday Boat Parade**
**Saturday, December 13, 2025 | 5:45pm - 8:00pm**
Just south of Venice, this spectacular event features dozens of decorated yachts and sailboats parading through the marina. This year's theme is "A Nightmare Before Christmas." The festivities begin with a light show at 5:45pm, followed by the parade from 6:00-8:00pm. Best viewing is from Burton Chace Park (13650 Mindanao Way), which offers free admission and food vendors. Arrive by 5pm for parking (mdrboatparade.org).

### Ongoing Activities

**Artists & Fleas: Venice Marketplace**
**Every Saturday | 11:00am - 5:00pm**
This outdoor market at Westminster Elementary School (1010 Abbot Kinney Blvd) features 50+ merchants selling art, vintage clothing, handmade jewelry, home goods, and design pieces. Food trucks provide lunch options, and the festive atmosphere intensifies during December with holiday gift options. Free admission; perfect for unique, locally-made gifts (artistsandfleas.com).

**Venice Beach Boardwalk**
Open year-round, the famous boardwalk maintains its eclectic energy through the holidays, with street performers, vendors, and artists adding festive touches to their displays. Muscle Beach, the outdoor gym, operates daily and is surprisingly busy even in December. Don't miss the Venice Skatepark adjacent to the beach for impressive displays of skateboarding skill (venicebeach.com).

**Venice Canals Walking Tour**
Self-guided walks through the historic canals (between Washington Boulevard and Venice Boulevard) are spectacular during the holidays, as residents decorate their homes, bridges, and yards with elaborate displays. The canals are free to visit 24/7, though evenings after dark (5pm-10pm) offer the best light displays. Respect private property and keep noise levels down in this residential area.

## Stay

### Hotels & Inns

**The Venice Beach House** ‚Äî This historic bed and breakfast, built in 1911 and originally designed by Julia Morgan associate Harriet Barnhart, sits just steps from the boardwalk. The nine rooms blend vintage charm with modern comforts, featuring period antiques, private baths, and some with ocean views. The garden courtyard and complimentary breakfast make it feel like a private residence. Rooms from $250-450/night during holidays; book early at (310) 823-1966 or venicebeachhouse.com.

**Hotel Erwin** ‚Äî A boutique hotel on the boardwalk featuring the acclaimed rooftop bar **Kassi Venice Beach**, which Eater LA dubbed "LA's hottest new restaurant and bar." Rooms offer ocean or canal views with modern d√©cor and private balconies. The rooftop is perfect for sunset cocktails with panoramic beach views. Rooms from $280-550/night; rooftop bar reservations recommended at (310) 452-1111 or hotelerwin.com.

**The Kinney Venice Beach** ‚Äî Offers a unique "Holiday Room Block Offer": book ten or more rooms by November 30, 2025, for stays between November 15, 2025, and January 31, 2026, and receive complimentary room upgrades (subject to availability), VIP welcome amenities, and late checkout. Ideal for family gatherings or friend groups celebrating together. Standard rooms from $189-350/night. Call (310) 821-4455 or visit thekinneyvenicebeach.com.

### Vacation Rentals

**Canals Cottages** ‚Äî Renting a cottage directly on the Venice Canals provides the most authentic local experience, especially during the holidays when you can watch the boat parade from your own deck. Rentals through Airbnb and VRBO range from $250-800/night depending on size and canal proximity. Book at least 2-3 months in advance for December dates.

## Shop

### Holiday Markets

**Venice Winter Fest** (November 22) serves double-duty as a holiday bazaar, with 300+ local designers, artists, and makers selling handcrafted jewelry, ceramics, vintage clothing, art prints, candles, and unique gifts. This is the best single-day shopping event in Venice (eventbrite.com/venicewinterfest).

**Artists & Fleas: Venice Marketplace** runs every Saturday through December, making it perfect for weekend gift shopping. The rotating vendors ensure new discoveries each visit. Located at Westminster Elementary School on Abbot Kinney Boulevard (artistsandfleas.com).

### Abbot Kinney Boulevard Shopping

Abbot Kinney Boulevard, named "coolest block in America" by GQ Magazine, offers approximately one mile of independent boutiques, galleries, and shops perfect for holiday browsing:

**Strange Invisible Perfumes** ‚Äî Luxury organic perfumes handcrafted in Venice since 1989. Create custom holiday scent profiles or choose from signature fragrances; $125-450 for perfumes (siperfumes.com).

**Burro** ‚Äî Curated home goods, apothecary items, and gifts from independent makers and designers. Think unique candles, ceramics, textiles, and kitchen accessories; $15-200 (burrogoods.com).

**Heist** ‚Äî Women's boutique featuring emerging and established designers with an edgy, California-cool aesthetic. Perfect for fashion-forward holiday gifts; $50-600 (heistvenice.com).

**Aviator Nation** ‚Äî Venice-born brand famous for its vintage-inspired hoodies, sweatpants, and beachwear in signature stripe patterns. The perfect California souvenir; hoodies $158-198 (aviatornation.com).

**Waraku** ‚Äî Japanese lifestyle shop specializing in ceramics, textiles, kitchen tools, and home goods. The minimalist aesthetic and carefully selected items make for sophisticated gifts; $20-300 (warakula.com).

**Small World Books** ‚Äî Independent bookstore since 1976, stocking curated selections of fiction, poetry, art books, and local interest titles. Holiday hours extended in December (smallworldbooks.com).

### Lincoln Boulevard Finds

**Surfside Venice** ‚Äî Local surf shop with boards, wetsuits, beach gear, and Venice-branded apparel. Open since 1962, it's a piece of Venice history (surfsidevenice.com).

**General Store** ‚Äî Design-focused shop offering clothing, home goods, and carefully selected lifestyle products with California minimalism (shop-generalstore.com).

## Takeaway

Venice Beach's holiday season delivers a uniquely Southern California blend of beachside bohemia and festive celebration. From light-filled canal boat parades to Abbot Kinney's boutique shopping and rooftop sunset cocktails, this neighborhood proves you don't need snow to feel the holiday spirit. 

**Pack smart:** Layers for temperature shifts between beach and evening, comfortable walking shoes for boardwalk and canal strolls, sunglasses even in December, and a reusable water bottle for staying hydrated during market browsing.

**Pro tip:** Combine your visit with the Marina del Rey boat parade (December 13) and Venice Canals parade (mid-December) for maximum holiday spectacle. Park once near Washington Boulevard and you can walk between both locations. The magic hour in Venice during the holidays is sunset (around 5pm in December) when the golden light hits the canals and the holiday lights begin to glow.
